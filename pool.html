"Let's build a pool," or, "Honey there's a fiberglass pond in the backyard!"

A year ago when we moved into our current abode we talked about looking for a house with a pool. But like most people we sometimes have a hard time letting us have something good. So we bought a beautiful house that has everything except a pool. That is until now.

In the sping we started by visiting all the pool places. Most laughed when we said we wanted a pool this year. It seems that traditional concrete pool construction is a slow process. All the local dealers were booked solid until next year. Bummer.

Then Michele, who loves to research things on the internet discovered fiberglass pools. These are molded in a factory and set into the ground in one piece. This greatly shortens the construction time, and the thing has a 30-year garantee. Best of all they could do it this year. And, while it is more expensive up front, there are fewer long term care costs.

Ideally the construction phase of this project is 8 days. This is assuming there are no difficulties and no rain. We had two days of rain, and the installer was down for a day or so when his bobcat lost it's hydralic system. Phooey.

The pages that follow more or less cronicle the progress of this adventure. The pictures were taken with a cheap digital camera in 640x480 resolution. I converted the images to JPEG format in order to load them faster here.
