I don't like changes in my schedule. Perhaps this is some character flaw, perhaps it is genetic. Maybe I'm just goofy. At any rate I don't like it when things change. A small change in what I perceive to be my normal schedule can upset me for hours. For example my normal commute to work lasts about 40 minutes. This is door-to-door travel time. The rest of my daily schedule more or less depends on this commute time staying the same. Naturally traffic doesn't always cooperate with me and often my drive is an hour or longer. The rest of the day I feel harried and out of control. This obsession doesn't limit itself to little things like driving time either. My brother, who lives 1000 miles away, just informed us of his wedding date. Now my whole year is shot. I have to use vacation time in order to attend his wedding. Getting all the flight and driving times down is eating away at me already. In order to feel comfortable I need to have control over all these little facts. I want to put them all in order and glue them in place so I can start to feel safe with them. The worst part is I don't have any real control over them. Sure I could buy a ticket today, and make car and hotel reservations. But then it is out of my control again. Now I am at the mercy of total strangers. And mercy is a rare commodity in today's society.

Nuts.

So far in my life this obsession hasn't been too harmful. But now I feel it is starting to intrude. Given that I have to commute to work, and that the variance in driving time can approach 300% I have to learn to let go of the need to have control over time. I need to let go of the need to always be at work by 7, eat lunch at 12, and leave for home at 4 on the dot. Getting here at 9 works just as well. You just stay until 6. Same number of hours. Same place, same people, same everything. Only it isn't the same. Part of this, as far as work is concerned, has to do with the energy of the place. Getting there early usually means I am the first person through the door. The building is quiet and empty. I get to fill it with my energy. Being the first to work as always been popular with me. In each of my previous 4 jobs I have managed to alter my schedule from the normal 8 - 5 or 9 -6 hours, to hours of my own choosing. Usually I am at my desk by 7 am. For a period of time I was getting to work at 6 am. Thinking back over the years I can distinctly remember times when I would arrive at 6 or 6:30 and discover someone else had "beaten" me there. I hated those days. My time alone at work had been invaded. I had lost control of the situation.

Maybe I don't like people all that much. I have always enjoyed working on Saturday when there was no one else in the building. I relish the idea of have it all to myself. I can control the lighting and climate without some one altering my settings. I've never had an office of my own with a door. I think that would be ideal for me. No matter when I got to work I could isolate myself from the intrusions of others. ("If the door is shut it means I don't want to see you.") Currently I share a u-shaped cubical that faces another row of u-shaped cubicles. Privacy is a myth. I hate the feeling of being exposed and of being on display. Other than having my own office with a door, I think working at home and tele-commuting is the best answer for me. I still get to interact with my peers, but I have lots of control over my immediate environment.
