For just over two years now, Michele and I have been working towards being debt free. This is a relative term mind you, for us it means no revolving credit. The only money we owe is for our car and our house.

How'd we do it you ask? Consumer Credit Counseling Service. It's a not-for-profit organization that helps people get free of revolving debt. They sit down with you and determine how much you owe. This is a rather sobering experience. Sure you get the statements each month, but when was the last time you added them all up? Then they help you complete a budget sheet. They aren't interested in having you live as paupers. When we did this exercise our counselor actually increased the amount of money set aside each month for entertainment and dining out. Finally you arrive at a monthly payment for your debt. They collect this money each month and distribute it to all your creditors.

What's the catch? You have to give up all your credit cards. They hand you a set of sissors and let you cut them all in half. Why would you want to do this? The creditors, for the most part, suspend interest and finance changes as long as you are in good standing with this program. That's right - no interest and no finance charges.

We have had some struggles along the way. Eventually some of your creditors will report back to the credit bureau that you've entered in to a payment schedule program. Buying a new car with this on your credit isn't easy. It helps if you have a good relationship with the bank.

Having said all of that, nothing can match the feeling now that we are in a postion to pay off the remaining balance. Imagine not have any monthly revolving debt. No more "minimum payments", no more 19.8% interest (or worse), no more calls dunning us for money. And the icing on this particular cake? After 26 months of payments we are used to living with out the 300 bucks each month. It will be easy now to save that for the things we want to buy.

The most important lesson we've learned from this experience? Money isn't important. The things that money buys aren't that important either. Sure, food and shelter are good things. But who needs the latest and greatest of anything. We, as a society, have lost sight of the truly important things in our lives in our mad pursuit of what the advertisers would have us believe is important.

My wife and I have a saying, "in truth there is love, and in love there is truth." Money is not truth. It isn't based on anything but our faith in the government. It isn't real. We are much happier now that we've stop chasing the almighty dollar, and started following our hearts.
